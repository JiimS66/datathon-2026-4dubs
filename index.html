Environmental Justice & Health Disparities

Crucial Finding

This section investigates Environmental Justice (EJ) in U.S. air quality. We construct an Unhealthy Risk Index using pollutant-specific data and quantify community vulnerability using EJ percentiles.

The Core Finding: High-pollution counties are disproportionately concentrated in high EJ vulnerability groups. Furthermore, counties facing both high pollution and high EJ risk exhibit significantly elevated asthma prevalence, suggesting compounding health impacts.

Pollutant decomposition shows that PM2.5 and Ozone days are the dominant contributors to pollution burden in these high-risk communities.

Methodology Note

We applied feature aggregation and percentile-based stratification (Top/Bottom 20%) to isolate structural disparities. The focus is on interpretable risk segmentation rather than black-box prediction.
