Environmental Justice & Health Disparities

Crucial Finding

This section investigates Environmental Justice (EJ) in U.S. air quality. We construct an Unhealthy Risk Index using pollutant-specific data and quantify community vulnerability using EJ percentiles.

The Core Finding: High-pollution counties are disproportionately concentrated in high EJ vulnerability groups.

Methodology Note

We applied feature aggregation and percentile-based stratification (Top/Bottom 20%) to isolate structural disparities.

Asthma Prevalence & Vulnerability

Key Insight: The "Double Burden"

Data indicates that vulnerable communities (High EJ) suffer higher asthma rates even at moderate pollution levels, but the gap widens drastically in high pollution areas.
